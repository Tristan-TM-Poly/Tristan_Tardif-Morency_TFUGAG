# TFUGAG Canon Map

## Purpose

This map separates the repository into layers so that TFUGA material can mature without mixing metaphor, hypothesis, code, and demonstrated results.

## Layer 0 - Stable trunk

Stable trunk material must be compact, reusable, and low-risk.

Current candidates:

- DCT-Omega status system: `S/E/X/D/C/A`.
- PowerScore as a qualitative prioritization heuristic.
- Air-Gap rule: AI-generated material crosses into public space through pull requests and review.

## Layer 1 - Crystallizable branches

Crystallizable material has enough structure to become formal, but still needs validation.

Candidate branches:

- TFUGA as a generalized research architecture.
- HGFM as a hypergraph/fractal/mycelial organization metaphor or model.
- OAK as a constraint-filtering operator.
- Bridge Engine as a method for mapping analogies across domains.
- Knowledge Atom as a bounded reusable research unit.

## Layer 2 - Exploratory branches

Exploratory material is fertile but not yet demonstrated.

Examples:

- Universal unification claims.
- Sedenion-based routing or cognition models.
- Direct equivalence between quantum collapse and language-model hallucination.
- Autonomous invention generation.

These should remain labelled `S` or `E` until they have definitions, assumptions, and tests.

## Layer 3 - Executable kernels

Executable kernels are small programs that make a claim testable.

Minimum standard:

- one function;
- one docstring;
- one example;
- one test;
- no hidden credentials;
- no network side effects by default.

## Layer 4 - Publication candidates

Publication candidates must include:

- abstract;
- claim status;
- assumptions;
- related work placeholders;
- validation path;
- limitations;
- reproducibility notes.

## Promotion logic

```text
S: interesting intuition
E: clarified exploratory concept
X: crystallizable with definitions/equations/test plan
D: demonstrated locally with proof, simulation, or calculation
C: canonical reusable component
A: archived or superseded
```
