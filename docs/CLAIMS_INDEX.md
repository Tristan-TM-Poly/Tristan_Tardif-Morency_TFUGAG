# TFUGAG Claims Index

## Purpose

This index prevents strong claims from becoming public canon before they are defined, tested, and classified.

Every significant TFUGA claim should be tracked here before it is promoted.

## Claim status scale

- `S`: speculative
- `E`: exploratory
- `X`: crystallizable
- `D`: demonstrated locally
- `C`: canonical
- `A`: archived

## Minimum claim packet

Each new claim should include:

```text
ID:
Name:
Status:
Plain-language claim:
Formal definition or placeholder:
Assumptions:
Evidence:
Counterexample or falsification path:
Risks if overstated:
Next action:
```

## OAK review questions

1. Is this a metaphor, a hypothesis, a demonstrated result, or a deployment claim?
2. Does the claim have a precise object?
3. Can it be tested or falsified?
4. What evidence would change the status?
5. What publication risk exists if the claim is overstated?

## Promotion rule

No claim moves to `D` without a proof, calculation, benchmark, simulation, or reproducible command. No claim moves to `C` without stable reuse and low duplication.
